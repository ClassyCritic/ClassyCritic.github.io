Current Iowa caucus projections

Last updated 1/17/16

With the Iowa caucuses coming up quickly, Donald Trump and Ted Cruz will need to make quite a swing in the polls for either of them to feel confident. Trump currently has a 71% chance of beating Cruz in Iowa, but Trump shouldn't feel comfortable just yet.

The Democrats have been in a heated battle in Iowa as well. While Clinton has a 71% chance of winning the state, the most recent poll has her a long ways ahead of Bernie Sanders (remember that these projections are median based). While Bernie has been competing in the state, he will also need to make a strong push in order to pass Clinton.

Current political projections

Last updated 1/11/16

The current GOP leader remains Donald Trump. As each competitor rises and falls, Trump has maintained his position on top of the polls. Donald Trump currently has a 97% chance of winning the Republican nomination, with Ted Cruz in second at 1.7%. The other candidates lack even further behind, with all other candidates below a 1% chance of winning.

With the latest IBD poll, Bernie Sanders is inching even closer to Hillary Clinton. Sanders currently has a 15.6% chance of beating Clinton, his best showing this season. While Clinton still has a handsome lead at 83.87%, Sanders has made a strong push for the top spot. (Remember this percentages fluctuate greatly, and become more accurate as election season approaches.)
